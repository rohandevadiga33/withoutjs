Our Courses

Automobile Engineering

Automobile Engineering deals with the study of various aspects of vehicles such as design, manufacturing, maintenance, and operation. Students learn about automotive systems, engine design, vehicle dynamics, and safety measures.

Civil Engineering

Civil Engineering involves the design, construction, and maintenance of infrastructure projects such as buildings, roads, bridges, and dams. Students study subjects like structural engineering, transportation engineering, environmental engineering, and geotechnical engineering.

Computer Science & Engineering

Computer Science & Engineering focuses on the study of computer systems, software development, and programming languages. Students learn about algorithms, data structures, artificial intelligence, and computer networks.

Electrical & Electronics Engineering

Electrical & Electronics Engineering deals with the study of electrical systems, circuits, and devices. Students learn about power generation, transmission, and distribution, as well as electronics, control systems, and communication systems.

Electronics & Communication Engineering

Electronics & Communication Engineering focuses on the design and development of electronic devices and communication systems. Students study subjects like digital electronics, signal processing, microwave engineering, and wireless communication.

Mechanical Engineering

Mechanical Engineering involves the study of machines, engines, and mechanical systems. Students learn about thermodynamics, fluid mechanics, material science, and manufacturing processes.

Mechatronics Engineering

Mechatronics Engineering combines elements of mechanical engineering, electronics, and computer science. Students learn about robotics, automation, sensors, and control systems.

Printing Technology

Printing Technology deals with the study of printing processes, techniques, and equipment. Students learn about offset printing, digital printing, prepress operations, and packaging.
